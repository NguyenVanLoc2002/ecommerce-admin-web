# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fashion shop staff (STAFF, ADMIN, SUPER_ADMIN) doing day-to-day operations: processing orders, confirming shipments, moderating reviews, managing inventory. They open this tool at the start of their shift or throughout the day to triage queues and keep the shop running. Context: desk or laptop, office lighting, business hours, task-focused not exploratory.

## Product Purpose

Internal admin tool for a fashion e-commerce operation. The goal on any screen is to reduce queue depth and resolve pending actions quickly. Success means fewer clicks to confirm an order, spot a low-stock alert, or resolve a pending review.

## Brand Personality

Capable, trustworthy, no-nonsense. The tool should feel like a competent colleague — it surfaces what matters, gets out of the way, and never cries wolf.

## Anti-references

- Gaudy SaaS marketing dashboards with animated hero numbers and gradient accents
- Glassmorphism panels that look great in dribbble shots and are hard to scan at speed
- Dark neon admin tools that look like security operation centers
- Overly playful B2C apps with celebration animations on every action

## Design Principles

1. **Urgency is legible at a glance.** Staff need to know in two seconds whether anything is on fire. Urgency signaling must be unambiguous, not decorative.
2. **Data density earns its space.** Every number, label, and row either helps a decision or doesn't belong. No ambient stats, no supporting copy that restates the title.
3. **Hierarchy through restraint.** What matters most gets more visual weight. Everything else recedes. The tool never shouts unless it has to.
4. **Reliability over delight.** Delight at specific moments (arriving data, completing an action) builds trust. Delight everywhere is noise.
5. **Actions are always one step away.** Every metric, panel, and alert links directly to the full list or form. The dashboard is a triage surface, not a destination.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigation for all interactive cards. Reduced-motion variants for animations. No color as the sole urgency signal (icon + color together).
